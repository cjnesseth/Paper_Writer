\title{Capping the Capacity Market: A Supply Function Equilibrium Analysis of Price Controls in PJM}
\author{}
\date{\today}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
% TODO: Rewrite abstract (Session 6). Draft from revision instructions:
% In response to record-high capacity auction prices, Pennsylvania Governor
% Josh Shapiro negotiated a \$325/MW-day price cap and \$175/MW-day floor for
% PJM's 2026/27 and 2027/28 delivery years, claiming \$21 billion in consumer
% savings. We analyze this intervention using a calibrated supply function
% equilibrium (SFE) model. Under the observed market structure---three pivotal
% suppliers with residual supply index below unity---equilibrium prices reach
% or exceed the Shapiro cap in constrained regions. The Shapiro settlement's
% lead-time argument misunderstands price functions: even at short lead times,
% prices affect expectations, retirement decisions, mobile resources, and
% information revelation. Suppressing prices below market-clearing recreates
% the ``missing money'' problem that capacity markets were designed to solve.
% The claimed \$21 billion in savings uses the VRR Point~(a) price as the
% counterfactual; our SFE model provides a structural estimate that is lower
% but still exceeds the settlement cap in constrained LDAs. Transmission
% expansion shifts constrained regions from cap-binding to interior-clearing
% regimes and dominates administrative price controls as a durable remedy.
\end{abstract}
