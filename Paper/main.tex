\title{Calibrated Supply Function Equilibrium Simulation of Market Power in PJM Capacity Auctions}
\author{}
\date{\today}
\maketitle

\begin{abstract}
Capacity markets are designed to ensure long-run resource adequacy, but when
only a few sellers hold pivotal capacity, the same auction mechanism that
compensates investment generates strategic market power. We quantify this
trade-off in PJM's Reliability Pricing Model using a calibrated Supply
Function Equilibrium (SFE) model. The equilibrium is selected by Holmberg's
boundary condition, which yields a unique SFE when capacity constraints bind,
and is calibrated to PJM's Variable Resource Requirement demand curve,
three-pivotal-supplier market structure, and an avoidable cost rate of
\$150/MW-day from the 2025 IMM State of the Market. Using seven years of BRA
data (delivery years 2021/22--2027/28) and planning parameters for all
constrained LDAs, we find Lerner indices of 0.54--0.65 at the baseline
$K = 3$ structure, implying equilibrium prices 2.2--2.9 times the competitive
level. The 2026/27 at-cap clearing price of \$329.17/MW-day is predicted
exactly without tuning, ruling out effective competition among four or more
symmetric sellers. Competitive outcomes require $K \geq 7$. At the LDA level,
import penetration (CETL relative to the reliability requirement) is the
dominant predictor of market power across 41 LDA-year cells. These findings
imply that existing binary mitigation rules understate markup severity, and
that transmission investment expanding import capacity is as much a
capacity-market policy as an offer-cap rule.
\end{abstract}
