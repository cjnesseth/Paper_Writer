\title{Price Suppression and Reliability Shortfall in PJM's Capacity Market}
\author{Christopher Nesseth}
\date{}
\maketitle
\thispagestyle{empty}

\begin{abstract}
\singlespacing
The January 2025 Shapiro settlement imposed a price cap of
approximately \$325 per megawatt-day and a floor of approximately
\$175 per megawatt-day on PJM's capacity auctions for delivery years
2026/27 and 2027/28, projecting consumer savings above
\$21~billion. The cap arrived in the two auctions with the most
concentrated residual supply and the tightest reserve margins in
the sample, raising the question of whether the projected savings
reflect transfers from inframarginal sellers or whether they also
suppress the price signal that would otherwise call forth new
capacity. With only a handful of collectively pivotal suppliers, a
binding ceiling truncates the equilibrium clearing point: price
stops at the cap and the procured quantity is read off supply
rather than off the administered demand curve, so the same
intervention that compresses revenue also reduces megawatts
acquired. To evaluate this mechanism, I calibrate a symmetric
supply function equilibrium to PJM's seven completed Base Residual
Auctions, using avoidable-cost data and the Independent Market
Monitor's residual supply estimates, and compare the model's
unconstrained equilibrium against the Monitor's mechanical
unrestricted-demand counterfactual. The unconstrained equilibrium
reaches the operative cap in both settlement years, confirming that
the ceiling binds. A reliability shortfall of 0.23\% in 2026/27 and
4.7\% in 2027/28 emerges in the capped auctions and in no prior
auction in the sample. The Monitor's counterfactual implies a
two-year revenue difference of \$13.08~billion and roughly
9{,}500~MW of additional capacity in 2027/28; the headline
\$21~billion figure conflates the price and quantity channels and
overstates pure consumer savings. Under any reading consistent with
genuine scarcity, the cap suppresses the investment signal in
precisely the years it would have been most informative.
\end{abstract}
