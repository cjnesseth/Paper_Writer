\section{Calibration}

% ------------------------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Data Sources}
\label{subsec:cal_data}
% ------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The calibration draws on four publicly available datasets produced by PJM
and the PJM Independent Market Monitor (IMM).

\paragraph{PJM Planning Parameters.}
PJM publishes the planning parameters for each BRA in advance of the
auction, including the VRR curve anchor points, Net CONE, CETL, and
reliability requirement for every LDA. These files are available in Excel
format and provide the complete VRR demand schedule that sellers observe
when submitting capacity offers. We processed the planning parameter files
for delivery years 2021/22 through 2028/29 using a custom parser that
extracts LDA-level parameters for each VRR design vintage and handles the
three-point (old) and four-point (new) curve specifications separately.
Spot-checks against published PJM summary tables confirm accuracy (e.g.,
2023/24 RTO Net CONE = \$274.96 per MW-day).

\paragraph{PJM BRA Results.}
Following each auction, PJM releases clearing prices and quantities by LDA.
We processed seven completed auctions (2021/22 through 2027/28); the
2028/29 BRA had not yet been conducted at the time of data collection.
Clearing results are verified against PJM press releases (e.g., 2025/26
RTO clearing = \$269.92 per MW-day; 2026/27 RTO clearing = \$329.17).

\paragraph{PJM IMM State of the Market Reports.}
The PJM IMM publishes an annual State of the Market (SotM) report containing
auction-by-auction summaries of market concentration, TPS test outcomes, and
offer-curve characteristics. We extracted Residual Supply Index values and
participant counts from the relevant report volumes using a PDF-parsing
pipeline. Volume 2022 covers delivery years through 2022/23, and Volume 2025
covers delivery years 2023/24 through 2027/28. Due to layout variation
across report vintages, RSI data is available for a subset of LDAs and
delivery years, as documented in Table~\ref{tab:tps_results}. The 2021/22
delivery year predates our RSI extraction window and is excluded from the
market structure analysis.

\paragraph{Auction Lead Times.}
BRA settlement dates and delivery-year start dates were coded manually from
PJM auction notices and verified against the data in
Table~\ref{tab:bra_timeline}. Lead time is defined as the number of calendar
months between the BRA settlement date and the first day of the delivery
year. The highly irregular lead-time pattern documented in
Section~\ref{subsec:inst_leadtime} is incorporated as a covariate in the
calibration of the investment-signal environment.

The four datasets are joined at the LDA--delivery-year level to produce a
master panel with 110 observations spanning 17 LDAs and seven delivery years
(2021/22 through 2027/28).

% ------------------------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{VRR Curve Parameters}
\label{subsec:cal_vrr}
% ------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Table~\ref{tab:vrr_params} reports the VRR curve parameters for the RTO
under both the old and new designs for the six delivery years with complete
BRA data. Under the old design, Point~(a) equals $1.5 \times \text{Net CONE}$
and Point~(b) equals $0.75 \times \text{Net CONE}$ by construction, so the
ratio $p_{a} / p_{b} = 2$ is exact for all old-design years except 2025/26,
where a transitional administrative cap produced a Point~(a) price above the
standard $1.5 \times \text{Net CONE}$ multiple.\footnote{The 2025/26 Net CONE
of \$228.81 per MW-day implies a standard cap of \$343.22, but the published
Point~(a) was \$451.61. This discrepancy reflects the fact that VRR anchor
points are fixed at planning-parameter publication and may diverge from the
final Net CONE if energy and ancillary service revenues are revised
substantially between publication and auction. We use the published anchor
points as the realized VRR parameters in all calibrations.}
Under the new design, Points~(b) and~(c) equal the explicit floor price of
approximately \$177--\$179 per MW-day, and Point~(a) is set at approximately
$1.5 \times \text{Net CONE}$.

The panel encompasses 110 LDA--delivery-year cells with observed clearing
prices across 17 LDAs. The three benchmark calibration years used throughout
Sections~\ref{sec:model} and~\ref{sec:results} are selected to represent
three distinct capacity market regimes: \textit{2023/24}, a low-price year
under the old VRR design with no evidence of strategic withholding
(\$34.13 per MW-day, $\mathrm{RSI}_3 = 0.68$); \textit{2025/26}, a
high-price year under the old VRR design that preceded the redesign
(\$269.92 per MW-day, $\mathrm{RSI}_3 = 0.62$); and \textit{2026/27},
the first auction under the new VRR design, which cleared at exactly the
Point~(a) cap (\$329.17 per MW-day, $\mathrm{RSI}_3 = 0.64$). Together,
these three years span the full range of price outcomes in the sample and
allow the comparative static analysis to isolate the effect of VRR design
changes from variation in market structure and cost parameters.
