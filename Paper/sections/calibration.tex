% ==============================================================================
% Section: Data and Calibration
% ==============================================================================

\label{sec:calibration}

The calibration draws on four publicly available datasets. PJM publishes
VRR curve anchor points, Net CONE, CETL, and reliability requirements
in advance of each BRA, and clearing prices and quantities by LDA after
each auction. The IMM reports Herfindahl-Hirschman Indices (HHI), Residual
Supply Indices (RSI), and Capacity Performance offer-cap composition in its
annual State of the Market reports. I join the four datasets at the
LDA delivery year level into a master panel covering all seven completed
BRAs, 2021/22 through 2027/28.

Table~\ref{tab:vrr_params} reports the RTO VRR parameters. Under the old
three-point design, Points~(a) and~(b) equal $1.5 \times$ and
$0.75 \times$ Net CONE respectively, with a transitional administrative
cap in 2025/26. Under the new four-point design (2026/27 onward),
Points~(b) and~(c) collapse to the floor price of \$177--\$179/MW-day
and Point~(a) is set by FERC Order ER25-1357.

I set $K = 3$ symmetric strategic sellers based on the IMM's
RSI \citep{MonitoringAnalytics2025_sotm}. Across the six sample years
with published IMM RSI data (2022/23 onward, since the 2021/22 BRA
predates the available IMM panel), $\mathrm{RSI}_3 < 1$ at the RTO
level (Table~\ref{tab:tps_results}). The three largest sellers are
collectively pivotal, meaning no competitive outcome is supportable
without their participation. RTO HHI exceeds 2{,}500 in every sample year, and IMM reports
indicate still higher concentration in many constrained LDAs
\citep{MonitoringAnalytics2025_sotm}. These values qualify as
highly concentrated under the joint DOJ/FTC Merger
Guidelines, which classify markets above an HHI of 2{,}500 as
highly concentrated \citep{DOJFTC2023_merger_guidelines}.
Sensitivity of the at-cap conclusion to $K$ is bounded by
Section~\ref{subsec:theory_sfe}, where at $K = 4$ the at-cap regime
breaks.

The constant marginal cost is set to $c = \$150$/MW-day, the
combined-cycle sector average reported in the 2025 IMM State of the
Market \citep[Table~7-38, \$149.32, rounded]{MonitoringAnalytics2025_sotm}.
The fringe is modeled as an inelastic block offered at $c$ with capacity
$\bar Q_f$ equal to total installed capacity less the capacity of the
three largest sellers. The model is calibrated and solved for all seven
completed BRA delivery years.
