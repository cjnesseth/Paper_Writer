\section{Calibration}
\label{sec:calibration}

\label{subsec:cal_data}

The calibration draws on four publicly available datasets produced by PJM
and the IMM.
PJM publishes VRR curve anchor points, Net CONE, CETL, and reliability
requirements for every LDA in advance of each BRA. I compiled planning
parameter files for delivery years 2021/22 through 2028/29, covering both
the three-point (old) and four-point (new) curve designs.
PJM releases clearing prices and quantities by LDA after each auction, and
my sample covers seven completed auctions (2021/22 through 2027/28)
verified against PJM press releases.
Residual Supply Index values and participant counts are drawn from the
IMM's annual State of the Market reports (Volumes 2022 and 2025). RSI data
is available for a subset of LDAs and delivery years, as documented in
Table~\ref{tab:tps_results}.
Lead time is defined as the number of calendar months between the BRA
settlement date and the first day of the delivery year
(Table~\ref{tab:bra_timeline}).
The four datasets are joined at the LDA delivery year level to produce a
master panel with 110 observations spanning 17 LDAs and seven delivery years
(2021/22 through 2027/28).

\label{subsec:cal_vrr}

Table~\ref{tab:vrr_params} reports the RTO VRR parameters for the six
delivery years with complete BRA data. Under the old design, Points~(a)
and~(b) equal $1.5 \times \text{Net CONE}$ and $0.75 \times \text{Net CONE}$
by construction, so the ratio $p_{a} / p_{b} = 2$ holds exactly in every
old-design year except 2025/26. In that year, a transitional administrative
cap fixed Point~(a) at \$451.61, above the $1.5 \times \text{Net CONE}$
multiple of \$343.22; VRR anchor points are fixed at planning-parameter
publication and may diverge from the final Net CONE. I use the published
anchor points throughout. Under the new design, Points~(b) and~(c) collapse
to the floor price of \$177--\$179/MW-day, and Point~(a) is set by FERC
Order ER25-1357. The Point~(a) values for 2026/27 (\$329.17) and 2027/28
(\$333.44) are not simple Net CONE multiples; the settlement cap of
\$325/MW-day therefore binds \$4.17 and \$8.44 below them.

\label{subsec:cal_market_structure}

The PJM IMM reports LDA-level Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI) values in its
annual State of the Market reports, and concentration is consistently high.
The RTO HHI exceeds 2,500 in every sample year, and most constrained LDAs
exceed 4,000---thresholds the Department of Justice classifies as highly
concentrated under standard antitrust benchmarks.

The key calibration input is the number of strategic sellers, $K$. I
calibrate $K$ against the Residual Supply Index with threshold three
(RSI$_3$). RSI$_3 < 1$ means the three largest sellers are jointly pivotal:
no competitive outcome is supportable without their participation. Across
the six sample years with IMM RSI data, the RTO RSI$_3$ is below 1
(0.62--0.73; Table~\ref{tab:tps_results}), which supports treating up to
three large sellers as jointly pivotal. The 2026/27 at-cap outcome is also
consistent with $K \leq 3$: the model predicts interior (below-cap) clearing
with four or more symmetric sellers in that year
(Section~\ref{subsec:res_concentration}). Cross-sectional LDA snapshots in
the 2022/23 IMM State of the Market show $\mathrm{RSI}_1 < 1$ for each of the
top three sellers in every constrained LDA, supporting $K = 3$ over $K = 2$
as the baseline.\footnote{The same 2022/23 snapshot reports single-supplier
pivotality ($\mathrm{RSI}_1 = 0$) in BGE and DEOK
\citep{MonitoringAnalytics2025_sotm}; a symmetric $K = 3$ calibration
understates local market power in these cases.} I therefore set $K = 3$ as
the baseline strategic count and examine sensitivity via the $K$ comparative
static in Section~\ref{subsec:res_concentration}.

Because RSI data is available only at the RTO level for most delivery years,
I impose the same $K = 3$ structure on each LDA calibration and let
cross-LDA variation enter through the VRR parameters and the CETL-based
fringe capacity. This homogeneity is an acknowledged limitation. Where a
single supplier is pivotal, the effective $K$ is closer to one and the
symmetric $K = 3$ calibration understates local market power. Conversely,
in LDAs with $\mathrm{RSI}_3 > 1$, three suppliers are not jointly pivotal
and $K = 3$ overstates concentration. LDA-level Lerner indices should
therefore be read as applying a common structural assumption to
heterogeneous local conditions, not as LDA-specific estimates.

\label{subsec:cal_cost}

The ACR for each strategic seller is set to $c = \$150$/MW-day, the
combined-cycle sector average reported in the 2025 IMM State of the Market
\citep[Table~7-38, \$149.32/MW-day, rounded]{MonitoringAnalytics2025_sotm}.
Combined-cycle units are the dominant marginal gas technology in PJM's
current resource mix, so this sector average serves as a technology proxy
for the generic strategic seller's going-forward cost. PJM applies the TPS
offer cap itself on a unit-specific basis using each resource's Net ACR
\citep{MonitoringAnalytics2025_sotm, MonitoringAnalytics2025_bra2627};
plant-level cost data for a more granular calibration are not publicly
available. The symmetric-firm assumption sets all $K = 3$ sellers to the
same $c$ and abstracts from cost and capacity heterogeneity.

The ACR proxies opportunity cost rather than measuring it directly. True
avoidable cost depends on projected energy-market revenues, fuel hedges,
maintenance schedules, and the firm's financial structure---none observable
at the portfolio level. The sector-average ACR therefore abstracts from cost
heterogeneity across strategic sellers. Table~\ref{tab:cost_sensitivity}
varies $c$ over \$100--\$200/MW-day at $K = 3$; the qualitative
settlement-binding finding is robust across this range.

The range also serves as a bracketing device. A uniform ACR of \$100/MW-day
yields an upper Lerner bound of 0.70 in the 2026/27 at-cap equilibrium; a
uniform ACR of \$200/MW-day yields a lower bound of 0.39.
Heterogeneous-cost configurations with sector-average ACR in this range need
not produce a Lerner index strictly between these symmetric bounds---the
symmetric SFE is not a proven envelope. The bracketing analysis nevertheless
establishes that the central Lerner estimate of 0.54 at $c = \$150$ is not
an artifact of a narrow cost assumption.

Fringe supply is modeled as an inelastic block offered at $c$. At the RTO
level, fringe capacity equals total installed capacity minus the three
largest sellers' capacity, scaled by $\gamma$ to explore fringe-size
sensitivity. For individual LDA calibrations, CETL---the maximum deliverable
import from the rest of PJM into the LDA---serves as the fringe proxy.
LDA-level CETL values by delivery year are drawn from the PJM planning
parameter files described in Section~\ref{subsec:cal_data}.

\label{subsec:cal_years}

I calibrate and solve the model for all seven completed BRA delivery years
(2021/22 through 2027/28), spanning both VRR design regimes and the full
range of observed market conditions. Five auctions (2021/22 through 2025/26)
were conducted under the old three-point VRR design; two (2026/27 and
2027/28) under the new four-point design with an explicit price floor. The
SFE is a static game in which sellers submit supply functions against the
VRR curve; the auction mechanism is structurally identical across all seven
years regardless of lead time, so no year is excluded on timing grounds.
