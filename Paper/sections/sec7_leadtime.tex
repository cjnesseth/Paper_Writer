\section{Evaluating the Lead-Time Argument}
\label{sec:leadtime}

% ------------------------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{The Complaint's Investment-Adequacy Theory}
\label{subsec:lt_theory}
% ------------------------------------------------------------------------------

The legal theory underlying the Shapiro complaint is an investment-adequacy
argument about auction design, not a market-conduct claim: Pennsylvania
alleged that the VRR price cap was miscalibrated given near-term entry
expectations, not that sellers bid strategically above competitive levels.
The SFE model addresses the second question: it confirms the 2026/27 at-cap
outcome is consistent with strategic pricing by $K \leq 3$ sellers. It does
not, however, address whether new entry could have responded to those prices
within the available lead time.

% ------------------------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{PJM's Lead-Time Record and the Settlement Horizon}
\label{subsec:lt_record}
% ------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Table~\ref{tab:bra_timeline} shows that BRA lead times compressed to as
little as 0.8 months for 2024/25 before partial restoration for the
settlement years: the 2026/27 BRA was held 10.2 months ahead and the
2027/28 BRA 16.8 months ahead. Both
horizons fall short of the 36--48 months needed for greenfield gas
generation, but 10.2 months is non-trivial for resources with shorter
deployment horizons of 6--18 months, including demand response, battery storage,
and capacity upgrades to existing plants.

% ------------------------------------------------------------------------------
\subsection{Why Prices Still Convey Information}
\label{subsec:lt_channels}
% ------------------------------------------------------------------------------

Even if greenfield entry is infeasible within 10 months, auction prices
inform decisions beyond direct construction: retirement deterrence (high
prices reduce the probability that marginal capacity retires), mobile
resource deployment (demand response and storage registrations respond to
financial incentives on short timescales), portfolio contracting (bilateral
capacity contracts reference BRA outcomes), regulatory and interconnection
signaling (sustained high prices prompt permitting and transmission approvals),
and forward expectations (investors planning for 2028/29 and beyond observe
current BRA outcomes to form priors about future price levels).
Retirement signaling is the clearest of these margins: the capacity market's
investment-inducement function includes price signals that deter premature
retirement of units that can still provide capacity when it is most needed
\citep{Cramton2005_capacity}. A two-year cap disrupts this signal precisely
in the years when scarcity is most acute.
The appropriate structural remedy is schedule restoration, which PJM is
pursuing, not price suppression, which removes the price instrument the
market design was built to use.
