## 4. Add Limitations Paragraph

Insert a new subsection 9.4 titled "Limitations" before Section 10:

> **9.4 Limitations**
>
> The findings rest on maintained assumptions whose validity cannot be fully tested. First, we assume K = 3 symmetric sellers with identical costs; actual PJM suppliers differ in technology, cost structure, and contract positions. The symmetric Lerner index should be interpreted as a central estimate, not a precise measurement. Second, the Holmberg boundary condition is derived for stochastic-demand markets; in the deterministic BRA, it is imposed as an equilibrium selection criterion. Alternative selection rules would yield different equilibrium prices. Third, our static model treats capacity as exogenous and cannot quantify entry distortions from the settlement. Fourth, we do not model TPS mitigation endogenously; the gap between predicted ($330) and actual ($34) prices in 2023/24 suggests mitigation effects our model does not capture. These limitations bound the precision of our quantitative estimates but do not affect the qualitative finding that the settlement cap is binding under concentrated supply.
